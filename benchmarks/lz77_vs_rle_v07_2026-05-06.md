# LZ77 vs RLE on VarDCT — empirical analysis (v07 sweep)

**Question:** Should the zenjxl encoder default to `lz77=False` (RLE) on
VarDCT for all effort levels, matching libjxl?

**TL;DR:** **NO.** RLE matches LZ77 for 98% of inputs (with no quality
change) and saves 25% encode time at effort 9 — but on **synthetic
gradients, RLE is 100-389% larger** than LZ77. The "match libjxl"
default would silently regress flat-gradient encodes by 5×. Either
keep `lz77=True`, or make it content-aware via the analyzer.

## Source data

- v07 sweep, lz77 axis: 1,125 matched (lz77=True, lz77=False) pairs
- Matched on (image, distance, effort, biters, ziters, gaborish, patches, pdl)
- All rows are VarDCT (lossy encodes, distance > 0)
- Distances: 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0
- Efforts: 5, 7, 9 (no e0-4 data — gap)

## Bytes are bit-identical for 98.5% of pairs

1,108 of 1,125 pairs have **identical** bytes — RLE and LZ77 happen
to produce the same code-length on photo HF coefficients.

The 17 non-identical pairs (1.5%) split sharply:

### LZ77 wins big on synthetic gradients (5/17 cases)

| image | d | e | bytes(T) | bytes(F) | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `synthetic__gradient_v_1024x1024.png` | 4.0 | 9 | 494 | 2,417 | **+389%** |
| `synthetic__gradient_v_1024x1024.png` | 4.0 | 7 | 613 | 2,419 | **+295%** |
| `synthetic__gradient_v_1024x1024.png` | 2.0 | 7 | 640 | 2,429 | **+280%** |
| `synthetic__gradient_h_256x256.png` | 2.0 | 9 | 332 | 720 | **+117%** |
| `synthetic__gradient_h_256x256.png` | 1.0 | 9 | 490 | 744 | +52% |

Flat gradients have long repeating HF coefficient patterns; LZ77 finds
multi-byte matches that RLE (a single-symbol run-length code) cannot.
Switching to RLE-by-default makes our gradient encodes **5× larger**.

### Screen/chart content: small effect either way (12/17 cases)

| image | range of Δbytes% |
|---|---|
| `size-dense-renders/4cd...` (chart) | -2.9% to +0.3% (RLE often wins) |
| `variants/cid22/StockQuoteGraph` (chart) | -1.2% to +1.6% (mixed) |

These are realistic web inputs (charts/UI screenshots). The deltas are
small and roughly balanced; either default ships fine for screen
content.

## Recommendation

| Workload | Recommended default |
|---|---|
| Photo-heavy | `lz77=True` (current) — costs 25% e9 speed, **never** loses bytes |
| Mixed web (charts, UI, photos) | `lz77=True` — small bytes win on screen content, big wins on gradients |
| Synthetic / generative content | `lz77=True` — required to avoid 4× regression on gradients |
| Pure photo + e9 latency-sensitive | `lz77=False` — only context where RLE wins meaningfully |

**Do NOT switch the global default to `lz77=False`.** The "match
libjxl" default silently regresses synthetic-content encodes by 100-389%
with zero quality change to balance it.

If e9 encode time is a problem, address it directly (skip the
exhaustive LZ77 search, not the LZ77 mode itself) or expose a
`fast_e9` knob that turns off LZ77 only when the caller opts in.

## Caveats

- **No e0-4 data.** Sweep only covered e5/e7/e9. The behavior at low
  effort is unmeasured. RLE may dominate at e0-2 where the LZ77 search
  is heuristic; needs sweep extension.
- **Sample size per cell:** 75 images per (effort, distance) cell;
  good for averages, weak for tail behavior.
- **Distance range 0.5-8.0.** No coverage at d<0.5 (near-lossless) or
  d>8 (extreme distortion); both edges may behave differently.
- Only zensim/ssim2/butter measured — no encode determinism check
  (lz77=True twice → same bytes? assumed yes).
